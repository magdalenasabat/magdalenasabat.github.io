I am a cognitive scientist studying collective behaviour and social tipping points in the context of the climate crisis.

My research revolves around *change*, moments when gradual shifts become collective transformations, when behaviours, norms, or opinions suddenly tip toward something new. 

I currently study early warning signals of social tipping points, particularly in relation to societal adaptation and mitigation responses to climate change. I combine behavioural experiments, formal modelling, and large-scale text data analysis to understand how individual decisions scale into societal change. This work is financed by the [FYSSEN foundation](https://www.fondationfyssen.fr) under scientific supervision of [Dr. Anne Urai](https://anneurai.net/) and Prof. [J.W. Bolderdijk](https://www.uva.nl/en/profile/b/o/j.w.bolderdijk/j.w.bolderdijk.html).

Before moving into research on human behaviour and climate, I trained as a neuroscientist at the École normale supérieure in Paris, where I studied auditory processing and neural dynamics under the supervision of [Yves Boubenec](https://sites.google.com/view/yves-boubenec/home) and [Catherine Tallon-Baudry](https://sites.google.com/view/tallon-baudry-lab/home). 

Privately I'm an impulsive pickler. I advocate for slow and open science. I am also a co-founder of Green Wave, a non-profit initiative dedicated to research and education on the biodiversity crisis and pathways for collective action.
